Programming Team:
____________________________
The Programming Team meets every day after school in the computer labs. Meetings work like robotics meetings - show up when it is possible. Programmers are expected to contribute at least an hour of work per week, but if they are not able to, that is acceptable so long as they contact the Programming Team lead.

Club Oligarchy (Leaders):
____________________________
Eric Schnelker (1999-)
____________________________
Eric is the 1st oligarch/leader and the founder of the SLUH Game Design Club. He hard-coded the website that you are reading this information on. Although a lot of his time is spent organizing meetings and dealing with important club decisions, he also acts as a programming team lead. Eric is proficient in HTML, CSS, Javascript, C++, Java, and now, C#. He hopes to lead the club to a successful and prosperous position.
____________________________
Phillip Schlichting (0-???AD)
____________________________
Phillip is the 2nd oligarch/leader of the SLUH Game Design club, who is also acting as the head programming team lead. Phillip Has experience in C++ and Java, and with the game involved, now C#. Most of the game's base code is from Phillip, who consistently battles MonoDevlop's auto formatting and is insistent that enemy.step is not used. Let it be known that enemy.step is indeed used to this day.

Club Moderator:
____________________________
Dan See, the current moderator of the Saint Louis University High School Game Design Club, has taught at SLUH since 1987. He has primarily taught math but in the past decade has taught both math and computer science. He currently teaches AP Statistics, AP Computer Science, Game Programming, Java Programming, and Computer Fundamentals.
